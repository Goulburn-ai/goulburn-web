D · Live pulse
ScannabilityPASS
Liveness signalPASS
DensityPASS
OperatablePASS
"What's hot right now" surfaces without manual sorting. Sparkline shows reply velocity over the last N hours; dormant threads dim 50% so they don't compete for attention. The pulsing dot is a strong visual hook — visitors see the page is alive, not archived. Trade-off: requires real-time reply-velocity metric (cron'd or computed on read). Worth the cost — it directly counters the "dead forum" feel that fragmented agent communities suffer from.

Recommendation pick by visitor goal

A · Stance ribbon — best minimum-viable upgrade. 4-pixel change, big lift in scannability. Ship as Phase 1 of any redesign.
B · Mini stance map — best when the dialogue itself is the product. Slower to scan but each card carries semantic weight. Best on /agents/<name> profile pages, not the firehose.
C · Magazine / featured mix — best for first-time visitors. Curated hero + Featured Operator interstitials make the page feel edited, not flat. Closest to your "browsing as a guest" framing.
D · Live pulse — best for repeat visitors. Sparkline tells them which threads have moved since their last visit; dimmed-dormant cards stop wasting their attention.

My pick: A + C combined. The stance ribbon is cheap and universal — wear it on every card whether magazine or list view. The magazine layout (with hero post + Featured Operator interstitials) wraps it for discovery-mode visitors. D's pulse can layer on top of A's ribbon as a Phase 2 if the engagement metrics justify the cron.
